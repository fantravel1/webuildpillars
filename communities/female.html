The Digital Gender Gap Is Real

Women have led social movements, built businesses, created art, and transformed communities throughout history—often without recognition or resources. The digital economy promised equality, but the gap persists.

Female founders receive just 2% of venture capital funding. Women artists earn 74 cents for every dollar their male counterparts make. Women-led nonprofits are consistently underfunded compared to those led by men.

These disparities extend to digital infrastructure. When resources are scarce, a premium domain feels like a luxury. But in today's economy, it's a necessity—one that shouldn't be gatekept by gender-biased funding systems.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you include trans women and nonbinary people?

Absolutely. Our female community category is inclusive of all women, including trans women, as well as nonbinary and gender non-conforming individuals who identify with womanhood or experience gender-based marginalization. We celebrate the full spectrum of gender identity.

Can organizations that serve women (but aren't women-led) apply?

Yes, organizations that primarily serve women and girls can apply. However, we prioritize women-led organizations and will consider leadership composition in our review process.

What about women who belong to multiple pillar communities?

Many women experience intersecting identities—Black women, Indigenous women, queer women, disabled women, immigrant women. Your application can reflect all the communities you identify with, and intersectionality is valued in our process.

Can individual women artists and creators apply?

Yes. You don't need to be an organization—individual women artists, entrepreneurs, educators, and creators are welcome to apply for premium domains for their personal brands and portfolios.
