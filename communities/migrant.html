Building Across Borders

Immigrants are among the most entrepreneurial people on Earth. Leaving everything familiar behind takes courage—and that same courage drives innovation, business creation, and community building in new homelands.

Yet immigrant entrepreneurs face unique barriers: limited credit history, language challenges, unfamiliar business systems, and too often, discrimination. Refugees and asylum seekers face even steeper odds, rebuilding entire lives from scratch.

Despite these challenges, immigrants start businesses at higher rates than native-born citizens and contribute trillions to the economy. They deserve digital infrastructure that matches their determination.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you require proof of immigration status?

No. We do not ask for or require documentation of immigration status. We serve all immigrants, refugees, asylum seekers, and migrant community members regardless of their legal status. Your privacy and safety are paramount to us.

Can organizations that serve immigrants (but aren't immigrant-led) apply?

Yes, organizations that primarily serve immigrant communities can apply. We prioritize immigrant-led organizations but recognize that allied organizations play crucial support roles.

What about second-generation immigrants?

Yes. Children of immigrants who identify with the immigrant experience and work with immigrant communities are welcome to apply. Diaspora identity doesn't end at the first generation.

Do you serve immigrants in countries other than the US?

Yes. Migration is a global phenomenon, and we serve immigrant communities worldwide—in Europe, Australia, Canada, Latin America, and beyond. Anywhere people are building new lives.
