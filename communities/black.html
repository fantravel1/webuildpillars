The Digital Divide Is Real

Black entrepreneurs, artists, and organizations have always been builders—creating culture, driving innovation, and strengthening communities. Yet systemic barriers have kept premium digital real estate out of reach.

While Black Americans represent 14% of the U.S. population, Black-owned businesses receive less than 2% of venture capital funding. This funding gap extends to digital infrastructure, where premium domain names—often costing thousands—remain inaccessible.

The result? Brilliant Black creators using free subdomains while competitors command authoritative .com and .org addresses. We're changing that equation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can Black-owned businesses get a free premium domain?

Black-owned businesses, nonprofits, and creators can apply for a free premium domain through our application form. We review applications based on community impact, mission alignment, and organizational readiness. Domains we gift range in value from $500 to over $100,000.

What types of Black organizations qualify?

We support Black-led nonprofits, social enterprises, artists, musicians, educators, researchers, community organizations, and entrepreneurs. Both established organizations and emerging creators are welcome to apply. We prioritize those who demonstrate clear community impact.

Do I need to be a registered nonprofit?

No. While we serve many 501(c)(3) organizations, we also support individual artists, unincorporated community groups, and for-profit social enterprises with clear community benefit missions.

What's included beyond the domain?

Depending on your tier, you may receive DNS setup assistance, professional email configuration, digital strategy guidance, and ongoing support. Our goal is to ensure you can fully leverage your new digital asset.
