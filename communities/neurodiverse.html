Wired Differently, Working Brilliantly

Neurodivergent minds have always driven innovation—from Einstein's dyslexia to Temple Grandin's autism to countless undiagnosed inventors, artists, and visionaries throughout history. Yet the modern world often fails to accommodate different ways of thinking.

Neurodivergent creators face unique challenges: sensory-overwhelming workplaces, communication barriers in networking, and a digital world often designed without accessibility in mind. Many brilliant autistic artists, ADHD entrepreneurs, and dyslexic educators struggle to establish professional online presence—not due to lack of talent, but lack of resources and systems designed for neurotypical minds.

The digital economy should work for different minds, not against them. A premium domain is often the first step toward professional credibility that opens doors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a formal diagnosis to apply?

No. We recognize that many neurodivergent adults, especially those from marginalized communities, lack access to formal diagnosis. Self-identification is respected in our application process.

What neurodivergent conditions qualify?

We support creators across the neurodiversity spectrum, including autism, ADHD, dyslexia, dyscalculia, dyspraxia, Tourette syndrome, OCD, and other neurological differences. If you identify as neurodivergent, you're welcome to apply.

Is your application process neurodivergent-friendly?

We've designed our application to be as accessible as possible, with clear instructions, flexible formats, and no time pressure. If you need accommodations, please let us know and we'll work with you.

Can organizations that serve neurodivergent communities apply?

Yes. Both neurodivergent-led organizations and ally organizations that primarily serve neurodivergent communities are welcome. We prioritize neurodivergent leadership when possible.
