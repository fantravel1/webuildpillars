Many Cultures, One Mission

"Brown" encompasses a beautiful tapestry of communities—Latinx families carrying generations of tradition, South Asian entrepreneurs bridging continents, Middle Eastern artists preserving ancient crafts, Pacific Islander storytellers honoring their ancestors.

Yet these vibrant communities share a common challenge: despite representing the fastest-growing demographic segments in many countries, they remain underrepresented in premium digital spaces.

Latinx-owned businesses, for example, represent $800 billion in annual revenue, yet only a fraction own premium domains. South Asian tech professionals lead Silicon Valley companies but often see their community nonprofits using free website builders. It's time for digital equity to match economic contribution.

Frequently Asked Questions

What communities does "Brown" include?

Our Brown community category includes Latinx and Hispanic individuals, South Asian communities, Middle Eastern and North African (MENA) communities, Pacific Islanders, Southeast Asians, and mixed-heritage individuals who identify with these communities. We use "Brown" as an inclusive term while honoring the distinct identities within it.

Can I apply if I serve multiple communities?

Absolutely. Many organizations serve multiple pillar communities. A Latinx LGBTQ+ advocacy group, for example, would qualify under both Brown and Queer categories. Your application should reflect all the communities you serve.

Do you have Spanish or other language domains?

Yes, our portfolio includes domains that work across languages and some that are specifically in Spanish, Portuguese, and other languages. During application, you can indicate language preferences.

Can international organizations apply?

Yes. We serve Brown communities globally, not just in the United States. Organizations based in Latin America, South Asia, the Middle East, and elsewhere are welcome to apply.

Ready to Elevate Your Digital Presence?

If you're a creator, entrepreneur, or organization serving Brown communities, you may qualify for a premium domain gift worth thousands of dollars.
