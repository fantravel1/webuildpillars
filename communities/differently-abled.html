Disability Is Not Inability

One billion people worldwide live with disabilities—that's 15% of the global population and the world's largest minority group. Yet disabled people remain dramatically underrepresented in professional spaces, including the digital economy.

The disability community has always been innovative. Curb cuts, invented for wheelchair users, now help everyone from parents with strollers to delivery workers with carts. Text messaging began as technology for Deaf people. When you design for disability, you design for everyone.

Despite this track record of innovation, disabled entrepreneurs and creators struggle to access the resources they need. The employment rate for disabled adults is just 21%, and disabled-owned businesses receive a tiny fraction of contracts and investment. Digital infrastructure shouldn't be another barrier.

The Disability Community We Serve

From paralympic athletes to disability rights lawyers, from Deaf artists to chronic illness advocates—every disability, every ability.

Physical Disabilities

Wheelchair users, amputees, people with mobility impairments, and those with physical conditions affecting movement.

Deaf & Hard of Hearing

Deaf artists, ASL educators, hearing loss advocates, and organizations serving the Deaf community.

Blind & Low Vision

Blind entrepreneurs, low vision artists, accessibility consultants, and visual impairment organizations.

Chronic Illness

Advocates and organizations for conditions like lupus, fibromyalgia, ME/CFS, diabetes, and other chronic conditions.

Disability Rights Orgs

Advocacy organizations fighting for accessibility, accommodation, and civil rights for disabled people.

Invisible Disabilities

Creators and advocates with conditions not immediately apparent—chronic pain, autoimmune diseases, mental health conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of disabilities qualify for domain gifting?

We support creators and organizations across the full spectrum of disability, including physical disabilities, sensory disabilities (Deaf, blind, low vision), cognitive and intellectual disabilities, chronic illness, mental health conditions, and invisible disabilities. Both formally diagnosed individuals and self-identified disabled people are welcome to apply.

Is your application process accessible?

We've designed our application to be accessible, including screen reader compatibility, clear language, and flexible response formats. If you need accommodations—extra time, alternative formats, or assistance—please let us know and we'll work with you.

Do I need to prove my disability?

No. We do not require medical documentation or proof of disability. We trust self-identification and understand that many disabled people, especially those with invisible disabilities, face barriers to formal diagnosis.

Can allied organizations that serve disabled people apply?

Yes, organizations that primarily serve the disability community can apply. We strongly prioritize disabled-led organizations and those with significant disabled leadership, but recognize the role of allied support services.
