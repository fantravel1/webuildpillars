What a Nonprofit Website Really Costs

Most advice about "affordable nonprofit websites" focuses on cutting costs on the wrong things. Here's the truth: the expensive part isn't hosting or website builders—it's the domain.

A memorable, brandable domain like HelpingHands.org might cost $5,000 on the open market. A standard hyphenated alternative like helping-hands-charity.org costs $12/year but hurts your credibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should a nonprofit website cost?

A professional nonprofit website can cost as little as $20-50/year using free tools combined with a premium domain. The most expensive element is often the domain itself—which We Build Pillars gifts free to qualifying organizations. Without that gift, comparable domains cost $500-$100,000+.

What's the most affordable way to build a nonprofit website?

Start with a free premium domain from We Build Pillars, then use free hosting (GitHub Pages, Netlify, or Google Sites via Google for Nonprofits) and a free website builder or simple HTML template. Total cost: essentially free except for minimal annual domain renewal.

Can I build a professional nonprofit website for free?

Yes. Combine a free premium domain from We Build Pillars with free hosting and free design tools. The only ongoing cost is domain renewal ($10-20/year typically). This approach can give you a more professional result than expensive website builders with free subdomains.

Why is a premium domain worth more than expensive hosting?

Your domain is permanent and scarce. Hosting is abundant and interchangeable. A premium domain builds SEO authority and trust over years, while hosting providers can be switched anytime. Invest in the asset that appreciates: your domain.
