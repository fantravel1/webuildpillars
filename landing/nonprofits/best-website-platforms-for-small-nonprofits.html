Best Website Platforms for Small Nonprofits

The platform matters less than you think. Your domain matters more than you know.

What Actually Matters for Small Nonprofit Websites

Small nonprofits spend too much time choosing between Wix, WordPress, Squarespace, and other platforms—when the decision that actually impacts their credibility is the domain.

Here's the reality: you can switch platforms anytime. Google Sites, Wix, WordPress, Squarespace—they're all interchangeable. What you can't easily change is a poor domain choice.

YourMission.org on Google Sites looks more professional than yourmission.wixsite.com on a premium Wix plan. The domain carries the credibility, not the platform.

Platform Comparison for Small Nonprofits

Google Sites

Best for: Tiny nonprofits, non-technical users
Free through Google for Nonprofits. Dead simple editor. Supports custom domains. Limited but sufficient for most small organizations.

WordPress.org

Best for: Growing nonprofits, those with volunteers who know tech
Free software, but requires hosting. Maximum flexibility. Thousands of nonprofit themes. Steeper learning curve.

Carrd

Best for: Single-page sites, events, campaigns
Simple one-page websites. Pro plan ($19/year) supports custom domains. Perfect for organizations that don't need multiple pages.

Wix / Squarespace

Best for: Design-focused organizations with budget
Beautiful templates, easy editors. But custom domain plans cost $150-200+/year. Check for nonprofit discounts.

Netlify / GitHub Pages

Best for: Tech-savvy organizations or those with developer volunteers
Free, fast, unlimited bandwidth. Requires working with code or static site generators like Jekyll.

Notion + Super/Potion

Best for: Organizations already using Notion
Turn Notion pages into websites. Easy to update for non-technical staff. Some tools have monthly costs.

The Domain-First Principle

Whatever platform you choose, start with your domain. Here's why:

  • Platforms are temporary — You might start on Google Sites, move to WordPress, then try Squarespace. Your domain stays constant.
  • SEO builds on your domain — Search authority accumulates on your domain name, not your platform.
  • Email depends on domain — Professional email (team@yourmission.org) requires your own domain.
  • Links don't break — When you share your domain, it works regardless of which platform hosts it.
  • Premium domains are scarce — You can always find a new platform. Premium domains are limited.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best website platform for small nonprofits?

For most small nonprofits, Google Sites (free through Google for Nonprofits) is the easiest option. WordPress.org offers more flexibility for growing organizations. The platform matters less than your domain—start with a premium domain from We Build Pillars, then connect it to any platform.

Should small nonprofits use WordPress or a website builder?

Website builders (Wix, Squarespace) are easier for beginners but can get expensive. WordPress.org is free but requires hosting and has a steeper learning curve. For tiny nonprofits, Google Sites is often ideal—completely free and simple. For larger organizations, WordPress offers more growth potential.

How much should a small nonprofit spend on a website?

Small nonprofits can have a professional website for under $50/year. With a free premium domain from We Build Pillars and free hosting (Google Sites, Netlify), your only cost is domain renewal. Avoid spending hundreds on website builders when free options work just as well for most nonprofits.

What's more important for a small nonprofit: the platform or the domain?

The domain. Platforms can be changed anytime, but your domain is permanent. A premium domain like YourMission.org builds trust with donors regardless of which platform hosts your site. Secure your domain first, then choose a platform.

Can I switch platforms later without losing my website?

Yes, if you own your domain. Simply point your domain's DNS to the new platform and rebuild (or migrate) your content. This is why domain ownership is so important—it gives you freedom to move between platforms as your needs change.
