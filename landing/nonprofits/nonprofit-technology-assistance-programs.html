Technology Resources for Mission-Driven Organizations

Nonprofits face a paradox: they need professional technology to compete for donors and serve communities, but their budgets prioritize program work over infrastructure. Technology assistance programs help bridge this gap.

Many programs offer free or discounted software, hosting, and productivity tools. But one critical piece is consistently missing: premium domain names. That's where We Build Pillars comes in.

Why Premium Domains Are the Missing Piece

Most technology assistance programs focus on operational tools—email, documents, CRM. But they miss the foundational asset that makes everything else work: your domain.

Here's why premium domains are uniquely valuable:

  • Permanence — Software changes, but your domain is forever. It's the one constant in your digital presence.
  • Scarcity — Free hosting is everywhere. Premium domains are finite. The best .org names are already owned—unless someone gifts them.
  • Trust — Donors and foundations assess credibility through domain quality. A premium domain signals organizational maturity.
  • Appreciation — Unlike software that depreciates, premium domains often gain value over time as your SEO builds.
  • Independence — You're not locked into any vendor. Your domain works with any email, hosting, or website platform.

That's why We Build Pillars focuses specifically on domain philanthropy—filling the gap that other technology assistance programs don't address.

Frequently Asked Questions

What technology assistance programs are available for nonprofits?

Major programs include Google for Nonprofits (free Google Workspace, Ad Grants), Microsoft for Nonprofits (free/discounted Office 365), TechSoup (discounted software), and We Build Pillars (free premium domain gifts). Each program has different eligibility requirements and benefits.

How can small nonprofits afford technology?

Small nonprofits can access technology through donation programs (TechSoup, Microsoft, Google), free tools (GitHub Pages, Netlify, Canva), and specialized gifting programs like We Build Pillars for premium domains. Many enterprise-level tools are available free or deeply discounted to verified 501(c)(3) organizations.

Does We Build Pillars provide technology assistance beyond domains?

Our primary focus is premium domain gifting, but higher tiers include digital strategy guidance, SEO assistance, email setup support, and hosting recommendations. We also help organizations understand and access complementary programs like Google for Nonprofits.

What's the most valuable free technology for nonprofits?

A premium domain is often the most valuable single technology asset because it's permanent, builds SEO value over time, and establishes credibility with donors. Unlike other technology, premium domains are scarce—which is why We Build Pillars focuses specifically on domain gifting for underrepresented communities.

Do I need 501(c)(3) status for technology assistance programs?

Most major programs (Google, Microsoft, TechSoup) require 501(c)(3) verification. We Build Pillars also accepts grassroots organizations, social enterprises, and groups working toward formal nonprofit status—the key is serving pillar communities.
