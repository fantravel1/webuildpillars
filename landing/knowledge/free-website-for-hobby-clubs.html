Free Website for Hobby Clubs

Premium domains for community groups sharing passion and knowledge.

Build Community Around Shared Interests

Hobby clubs bring people together—teaching skills, sharing knowledge, and building friendships. But many clubs rely on social media pages that get buried in algorithms.

A professional domain like YourClub.org gives your hobby group a permanent, findable home. New members can discover you through search. Events are easy to share. Your club looks established and welcoming.

We Build Pillars gifts premium domains—valued at $500 to $100,000+—to hobby clubs and community groups serving underrepresented communities. Build your club's presence on a foundation that lasts.

What Your Club Website Can Include

  • About your club: Mission, history, and what makes your group special
  • Events calendar: Meetings, workshops, and special events
  • Membership info: How to join and what to expect
  • Photo gallery: Highlights from club activities
  • Resources: Tutorials, guides, and shared knowledge
  • Contact: How to reach club organizers
  • Blog: Updates, member spotlights, and club news

You don't need everything at once—start simple and grow your site as your club grows.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can hobby clubs get free domains?

Yes. We Build Pillars gifts premium domains valued at $500 to $100,000+ to hobby clubs and community groups that bring together underrepresented communities. A professional domain helps your club look established and attract new members.

What kinds of hobby clubs qualify?

We support clubs focused on creative hobbies, sports, gardening, gaming, collecting, crafts, outdoor activities, and any shared interest that builds community. The key is that your club serves or is led by members of underrepresented communities.

Does our club need to be a formal organization?

No. We support informal clubs and meetup groups alongside established organizations. You don't need nonprofit status or formal incorporation—just a group of people sharing a hobby and building community.
