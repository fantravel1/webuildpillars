Your Educational Project Deserves a Professional Home

Educational projects change lives—but too many operate with unprofessional web addresses that undermine their credibility.

A domain like YourProgram.org signals permanence and professionalism to students, parents, and funders. It's easier to remember, easier to share, and establishes your project as a serious educational resource.

We Build Pillars gifts premium domains—valued at $500 to $100,000+—to educational projects serving underrepresented communities. Whether you're building an online course platform, tutoring network, or educational resource library, we want to help you look as professional as your mission deserves.

Why Your Domain Matters

A professional domain does more than look good. It:

  • Builds trust — Parents and students take you seriously
  • Attracts funders — Grants go to organizations that look established
  • Stays permanent — Your URL never changes even if you switch platforms
  • Enables email — Get professional email like info@yourproject.org
  • Increases sharing — Short, memorable URLs get shared more

The domains we gift are valued at $500 to $100,000+. They're short, memorable, and professional—the kind of addresses that established institutions pay top dollar for.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can educational projects get free domains?

Yes. We Build Pillars gifts premium domains valued at $500 to $100,000+ to educational projects led by or serving underrepresented communities. From online courses to tutoring programs to educational games, your project can have a professional web address.

What kinds of educational projects qualify?

We support a wide range: online courses, tutoring programs, educational nonprofits, curriculum sharing projects, educational apps and games, literacy programs, STEM initiatives, arts education, and more. The key is that the project builds knowledge in underrepresented communities.

Does the project need to be a nonprofit?

No. While we support educational nonprofits, we also support individual educators, educational social enterprises, and community education initiatives. The focus is on the educational impact, not the organizational structure.
