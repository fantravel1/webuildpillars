Your domain is permanent. Hosting is flexible. Get the important part right.

The Domain-First Approach to Artist Websites

Independent artists need websites, but budgets are tight. The good news: you can build a professional artist website for free. The key is prioritizing what matters most.

Hosting platforms are interchangeable—you'll probably switch hosts multiple times in your career. But your domain is permanent. YourName.art stays the same whether you're on Squarespace, WordPress, or hand-coded HTML.

We Build Pillars gifts premium domains—valued at $500 to $100,000+—to artists from underrepresented communities. With a professional domain secured, you can use any free hosting platform and still look completely professional.

Why Your Domain Matters More Than Hosting

Consider this scenario:

  • janedoe.art hosted on Carrd's free tier
  • janedoe.carrd.co on the same platform

Same website. Same hosting. But the first URL looks professional, memorable, and permanent. The second looks amateur and temporary.

Curators, galleries, and clients notice. Media outlets prefer linking to clean URLs. And when you eventually upgrade to paid hosting or switch platforms entirely, janedoe.art stays exactly the same.

That's why we focus on gifting premium domains. The hosting part is easy to figure out later.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best free hosting for independent artists?

Popular free options include GitHub Pages (for static sites), Netlify, Vercel, and WordPress.com's free tier. Portfolio platforms like Adobe Portfolio (free with Creative Cloud) also provide hosting. But the more important question is your domain—that's permanent, while hosts can change.

Can I get both a free domain and free hosting?

Yes. We Build Pillars gifts premium domains valued at $500-$100,000+ to artists from underrepresented communities. You can then connect your domain to any free hosting platform like Carrd, GitHub Pages, Netlify, or WordPress.com.

Should I get hosting or a domain first?

Get the domain first. Hosting platforms come and go, and you'll likely switch hosts several times in your career. But your domain is permanent—it's the address people remember and the link that never breaks.
