Building Digital Equity for Black Entrepreneurs

The digital divide isn't just about internet access—it's about digital assets. Premium domain names represent real wealth in the digital economy, and that wealth has historically been concentrated in the hands of those who could afford to acquire it.

Black-owned businesses and Black-led organizations often start with limited resources, making premium domains seem like a luxury. But a memorable, brandable domain isn't a luxury—it's infrastructure. It's the foundation that makes everything else work: professional email, credible web presence, effective marketing.

We Build Pillars exists to redistribute digital wealth. We gift premium domains—valued at $500 to $100,000+—to Black-owned businesses, Black-led nonprofits, and organizations serving Black communities. No strings attached. No hidden costs. Just an investment in your success.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Black-owned businesses get free premium domains?

Yes. We Build Pillars specifically prioritizes Black-owned businesses, Black-led nonprofits, and organizations serving Black communities. We gift premium domains valued at $500 to $100,000+ as part of our commitment to building digital equity in pillar communities.

What types of Black-owned organizations qualify for free domains?

We support Black-owned social enterprises, Black-led nonprofits, community organizations, cultural institutions, advocacy groups, and mission-driven businesses. The key is that your organization either is Black-led or primarily serves Black communities.

Why does We Build Pillars focus on Black-owned organizations?

The digital divide disproportionately affects Black communities. Premium domain names—key digital assets—are concentrated among those with resources to acquire them. By gifting premium domains to Black-led organizations, we're redistributing digital wealth to build equity.

Do I need to be a registered nonprofit?

No. We accept applications from Black-owned social enterprises, community organizations, and even individual Black artists and creators. The key is mission alignment and community impact, not formal nonprofit status.

Are there ongoing costs after receiving a domain?

We cover the initial domain transfer. After that, you'll be responsible for annual renewal (typically $10-20/year for standard domains). The value of the gifted domain far exceeds these minimal ongoing costs.
