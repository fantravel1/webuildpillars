Supporting Digital Sovereignty

Indigenous peoples have always been storytellers, knowledge-keepers, and community builders. The digital world offers new ways to continue these traditions—but only if Indigenous communities can establish presence on their own terms.

A premium domain is more than a website address. It's digital territory. When an Indigenous organization owns NativeLandPreservation.org rather than being confined to a subdomain on someone else's platform, they control how their community is represented online.

We Build Pillars recognizes Indigenous peoples as a priority pillar community. We gift premium domains—valued at $500 to $100,000+—to organizations serving Native American, First Nations, and Indigenous communities worldwide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Indigenous organizations get free premium domains?

Yes. We Build Pillars recognizes Indigenous peoples as a priority pillar community. We gift premium domains valued at $500 to $100,000+ to Indigenous-led organizations, tribal entities, First Nations groups, and organizations serving Native communities.

What types of Indigenous organizations qualify?

We support tribal governments, Indigenous nonprofits, cultural preservation organizations, language revitalization projects, Indigenous arts organizations, Native American community centers, First Nations health services, and any organization led by or primarily serving Indigenous peoples.

How does digital presence support Indigenous sovereignty?

A strong digital presence allows Indigenous communities to tell their own stories, preserve languages and culture on their own terms, and engage with global audiences without intermediaries. Premium domains establish credibility and permanence for this important work.

Do tribal entities have special considerations?

Tribal governments and tribally-chartered organizations are automatically considered pillar community members. We understand the unique sovereignty status of tribal nations and work to support their digital infrastructure needs.
