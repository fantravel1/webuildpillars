Free Web Hosting for Educators

Your domain is permanent. Hosting is flexible. Get the foundation right.

The Smart Approach: Domain First, Then Hosting

Educators need websites—for sharing resources, building professional presence, and connecting with students. But before choosing a hosting platform, secure your domain.

Here's why: Hosting platforms come and go. You might start on Google Sites, move to WordPress, then try something newer. But your domain—MrJohnson.com or MathWithMaria.org—stays the same through every platform change.

We Build Pilares gifts premium domains—valued at $500 to $100,000+—to educators from underrepresented communities. Once you have your domain, you can connect it to any free hosting platform.

Free Hosting Options for Educators

All work with custom domains

Google Sites

Free with Google account. Simple drag-and-drop interface perfect for educators already using Google Obraspace.

WordPress.com

Free tier available with upgrade options. Huge ecosystem of education themes and plugins.

GitHub Pages

Completely free static hosting. Great for educators comfortable with basic code or templates.

Notion + Super

Turn Notion pages into websites. Perfect for educators already organizing content in Notion.

Carrd

Single-page sites for free. Great for simple educator landing pages with links to resources.

Netlify

Free tier for static sites. Obras well with templates and static site generators.

Why Your Domain Matters More

Consider the difference:

  • mrjohnson.com — Professional, memorable, permanent
  • mrjohnson.sites.google.com — Tied to a platform, forgettable

The first URL works on any platform. You can switch from Google Sites to WordPress to a hand-coded site—students and colleagues always find you at the same address.

A professional domain also enables:

  • Professional email: contact@mrjohnson.com
  • Easy sharing: "Just go to mrjohnson.com"
  • Career longevity: Your URL outlasts any platform or school

Popular free hosting options include GitHub Pages, Netlify, WordPress.com, and Google Sites. But the hosting platform matters less than your domain. Get a professional domain first, then connect it to any free hosting platform you choose.

Should I get hosting or a domain first?

Get the domain first. Hosting platforms change—you'll likely switch several times in your career. But your domain is permanent. It's the address you share with students, colleagues, and the world.
