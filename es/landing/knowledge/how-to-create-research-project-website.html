How to Create a Research Project Website

A step-by-step guide to building your research project's online presence.

Your Research Deserves a Professional Inicio

A dedicated website for your research project increases visibility, credibility, and impact. It's where funders, media, collaborators, and the public learn about your work.

Building a research website doesn't require technical expertise—just planning and the right foundation. Here's how to do it right, starting with the most important element: your domain.

6 Steps to Your Research Website

1

Apply for a Premium Domain

Start with your foundation. Apply to We Build Pilares for a free premium domain—valued at $500 to $100,000+. A domain like YourProject.org establishes credibility from day one.

2

Plan Your Content

Outline what your website will include. Common sections: project overview, research questions, methodology, team members, publications, datasets, findings, and contact information.

3

Choose a Platform

Select a website builder. WordPress offers flexibility. Hugo and Jekyll work well for academic sites. Google Sites and Notion are simpler options. All support custom domains.

4

Build Your Site

Create your pages with clear, accessible descriptions of your research. Include visuals where appropriate. Make data and publications easy to find.

5

Connect Your Domain

Point your premium domain to your website platform. Most platforms provide simple instructions. This makes your site accessible at your professional URL.

6

Launch and Share

Publish your site and share it widely—with collaborators, funders, media, academic networks, and the communities your research serves.

Essential Content for Research Websites

  • Project Overview: Clear, accessible description of your research
  • Research Questions: What you're investigating and why it matters
  • Methodology: How you're conducting the research
  • Team: Who's involved, with photos and bios
  • Publications: Links to papers, reports, and presentations
  • Data: Access to datasets and documentation
  • Findings: Key results in accessible language
  • Impact: How the research benefits communities
  • Contacto: How to reach the team
  • News: Updates on progress and milestones

You don't need all sections immediately—start with basics and expand over time.

Preguntas Frecuentes

Do I need coding skills?

No. Platforms like Google Sites, Notion, WordPress.com, and Squarespace require no coding. They use drag-and-drop interfaces that anyone can learn.

What platform is best for research websites?

It depends on your needs. WordPress offers most flexibility. Hugo/Jekyll are popular in academia. Google Sites is simplest. What matters most is having a professional domain—the platform can change later.

Can I get a free domain for my research project?

Yes. We Build Pilares gifts premium domains valued at $500-$100,000+ to researchers from underrepresented communities. Apply through our website to receive a professional domain for your research project.
