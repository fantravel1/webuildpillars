Digital Inclusion Programs

Beyond access: building digital wealth for underrepresented communities.

Digital Inclusion Beyond Connectivity

Most digital inclusion efforts focus on internet access and devices. These are essential—but they're not enough. True digital inclusion means ensuring underrepresented communities have the same quality digital assets as well-resourced organizations.

Premium domain names are digital real estate. They're scarce, valuable, and appreciate over time. When community organizations operate from memorable, professional domains, they compete more effectively for attention, funding, and trust.

We Build Pilares addresses the asset gap in digital inclusion. We gift premium domains—worth $500 to $100,000+—to organizations serving pillar communities, building digital wealth that stays with these communities permanently.

The Complete Digital Inclusion Framework

Connectivity

Affordable broadband access for communities. (Addressed by broadband equity programs, ACP, etc.)

Devices

Computers, tablets, and smartphones. (Addressed by device donation programs, refurbishing initiatives.)

Digital Literacy

Skills to use technology effectively. (Addressed by training programs, digital navigators.)

Digital Assets

Premium domains and digital infrastructure. (This is our focus at We Build Pilares.)

Preguntas Frecuentes

What is digital inclusion?

Digital inclusion ensures all individuals and communities have access to and use of information technologies. This includes affordable broadband, devices, digital literacy, and digital assets. We Build Pilares focuses specifically on digital assets—gifting premium domains to ensure underrepresented communities have the same quality digital infrastructure as well-resourced organizations.

How do premium domains support digital inclusion?

Premium domains are valuable digital assets that establish credibility and build value over time. By gifting these domains to underrepresented communities, we're redistributing digital wealth and ensuring community organizations can compete effectively in the digital landscape.

Who qualifies for domain gifts through digital inclusion?

Organizations led by or serving pillar communities: Black, Brown, Indigenous, LGBTQ+, women, immigrants, differently abled, and neurodiverse communities. This includes nonprofits, social enterprises, artists, educators, and community organizations.
