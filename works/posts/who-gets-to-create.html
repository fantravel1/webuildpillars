Who Gets to Create?

Examining the gatekeeping structures that determine whose creativity gets supported and celebrated.

The creator economy generates billions of dollars annually. But who captures that value? And more importantly, who gets permission to participate in the first place?

The 35% Pay Gap

Research consistently shows that creators from pillar communities—Black, Brown, Indigenous, Queer, neurodiverse, female, migrant, and differently abled artists—earn significantly less than their peers for equivalent work. The gap averages 35%, but in some creative fields, it's even wider.

This isn't about talent. It's about access. Access to networks, mentorship, funding, platforms, and—yes—professional digital infrastructure.

The Gatekeeping Stack

Between a creator and their audience sits a stack of gatekeepers:

  • Platforms: Algorithms that decide who gets discovered
  • Institutions: Galleries, labels, publishers that confer legitimacy
  • Funders: Grant-makers and investors who allocate resources
  • Media: Critics and publications that shape narrative
  • Infrastructure: The digital tools and presence that enable professional work

Each layer of this stack has historically been biased toward those with existing resources and connections. Breaking through requires not just talent, but the capital to project professionalism.

Digital Infrastructure as Gatekeeper

Consider what it means to contact a gallery or venue using a free email address versus your own domain. Or to share a portfolio on a subdomain versus your own professional URL.

These may seem like small differences, but they compound. A gallerist receiving 500 submissions makes snap judgments. An email from artist@sarahchen.art signals investment in craft. An email from sarahchen.artist123@gmail.com signals... less.

This is the hidden tax on creators from pillar communities: the constant need to prove professionalism that others have by default.

Breaking the Pattern

What if we removed one gatekeeping layer entirely? What if every talented creator from a pillar community had access to the same premium digital infrastructure as established artists?

That's why We Build Pillars focuses on premium domains. Not because a domain solves everything—it doesn't. But it removes one barrier, one excuse, one reason for gatekeepers to pass you over.

When you contact an opportunity from yourname.com, you're not asking for permission. You're arriving as an equal.

The Ripple Effect

When one creator breaks through, they open doors for others. They become mentors, collaborators, advocates. They change what's possible for those who come next.

Digital infrastructure isn't just about individual success—it's about building the scaffolding for entire communities to rise.
