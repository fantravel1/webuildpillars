Understanding CMS Systems

A guide to content management systems for nonprofits and creators building their digital presence.

Once you have your premium domain, you need somewhere to build. A Content Management System (CMS) is the software that powers your website. Choosing the right one can mean the difference between a site that grows with you and one that holds you back.

What Is a CMS?

A CMS is software that lets you create, manage, and modify content on a website without needing to code everything from scratch. Think of it as the engine under your website's hood.

Popular Options Compared

WordPress

Best for: Organizations needing flexibility and scale

  • Pros: Incredibly flexible, huge plugin ecosystem, strong SEO capabilities, owns your data
  • Cons: Steeper learning curve, requires maintenance and security updates
  • Cost: Free software, but hosting costs $5-50/month

Squarespace

Best for: Visual-first creators who want simplicity

  • Pros: Beautiful templates, all-in-one hosting, easy to use
  • Cons: Less flexible, can become expensive, you don't own the platform
  • Cost: $16-49/month

Wix

Best for: Small organizations with limited technical resources

  • Pros: Drag-and-drop simplicity, good for beginners
  • Cons: SEO limitations, can feel restrictive as you grow
  • Cost: $16-45/month for ad-free

Webflow

Best for: Design-focused organizations with some technical capacity

  • Pros: Powerful design tools, clean code output, good for portfolios
  • Cons: Steeper learning curve, more expensive
  • Cost: $14-39/month

Static Site Generators (Hugo, Jekyll, 11ty)

Best for: Technical teams prioritizing speed and security

  • Pros: Extremely fast, very secure, can host for free on GitHub Pages
  • Cons: Requires technical knowledge, harder to update for non-developers
  • Cost: Often free

Choosing the Right Fit

Ask yourself these questions:

  • Who will update the site? Technical team or non-technical staff?
  • What features do you need? Blog, donations, e-commerce, events?
  • What's your budget? Monthly costs add up over years
  • How important is ownership? Can you export your content if you leave?
  • What's your growth trajectory? Will this platform scale with you?

Our Recommendation

For most mission-driven organizations, we lean toward WordPress (self-hosted) or static sites for several reasons:

  • You own your content and can move it anywhere
  • No monthly platform fees eating into your budget
  • Maximum SEO control
  • Huge ecosystem of help and resources

That said, if you're a solo creator who needs to launch quickly and has budget for monthly fees, Squarespace or Webflow are solid choices.
