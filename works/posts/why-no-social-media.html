Why No Social Media?

Our intentional choice to build community outside the platform economy.

You might notice we don't have social media links in our footer. No Twitter/X handle, no Instagram, no TikTok, no LinkedIn. This isn't an oversight—it's a choice. And it's one we encourage our partners to consider.

The Platform Problem

Social media platforms promise reach and engagement. What they deliver is dependency. Consider what you're actually getting:

  • Rented audience: Your followers aren't yours—they belong to the platform
  • Algorithmic whims: Your visibility depends on ever-changing rules you don't control
  • Data extraction: Your community's attention and data are the real product
  • Constant churn: Platforms rise and fall; your investment can evaporate overnight

The Owned Alternative

We believe in owned infrastructure over rented presence. When you have:

  • Your own domain: An address that's yours forever
  • Your own email list: Direct connection to your community
  • Your own content: Work that lives on your terms

You're building equity, not just engagement.

What We Do Instead

Rather than chasing viral moments, we focus on:

  • Direct relationships: Email, contact forms, personal connections
  • Evergreen content: Articles and resources that remain valuable over time
  • Partner referrals: Word of mouth from organizations we've served
  • SEO and AEO: Being discoverable when people are actively looking

A Note for Our Partners

We're not saying social media is wrong for everyone. For many organizations and creators, strategic platform presence makes sense. The key word is strategic.

What we advocate against is platform dependency—putting all your digital eggs in baskets you don't own. Before investing heavily in any platform, ask:

  • What would happen if this platform disappeared tomorrow?
  • What would happen if the algorithm changed to hide your content?
  • Are you building something permanent, or just creating content for someone else's platform?

Your Domain as Home Base

Whatever you decide about social media, your own domain should be your home base. It's where you control the experience, own the relationship, and build lasting value.

Social media can be an outpost—a way to reach new people and direct them home. But home should be yours.
